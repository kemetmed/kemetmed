Frequently Asked Questions

What is healthcare technology?

Healthcare technology is any technology, including medical devices, IT systems, algorithms, artificial intelligence (AI), cloud and blockchain, designed to support healthcare organizations.

What is meant by HIPPA?

The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 (HIPAA) is a federal law that required the creation of national standards to protect sensitive patient health information from being disclosed without the patient's consent or knowledge.

What does health care companies will do?

A healthcare provider is an institution (such as a hospital or clinic) or person (such as a physician, nurse, allied health professional or community health worker) that provides preventive, curative, promotional, rehabilitative or palliative care services in a systematic way to individuals, families or communities.

What’s the difference between telehealth and telemedicine?

Telemedicine is the use of communications technology, specifically, to provide real-time virtual care and remote clinical services to patients. Telehealth refers to all the other ways technology like video meetings can be used to facilitate non-clinical healthcare services like billing, administrative meetings, education, and training.
